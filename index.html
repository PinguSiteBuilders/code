Reducing Our Carbon Footprint

How to Save the Planet


Why Does It Matter?

First of all, our carbon footprint is the amount of carbon emissions that we produce as a community. Excess carbon emissions can cause a plethora of problems for habitats across the globe from the melting of the poles to several droughts in the deserts. However, what does the environment matter to us? The damage carbon emmissions are doing to the environment probably won't affect us, and worst case we can just push the problem into the hands of the next generation, right?


Carbon emissions, as small as the problem may seems, has a global and lasting impact on our way of life and all other living creatures that breathe, eat, and live on this planet. Carbon emissions lead to more severe heat waves, floods, and droughts, damaging our home and other animal habitats. Heat waves reduce crop yields per acre, forcing us to use more land to support the ever growing population of humanity. This then leads to less plants that can intake carbon dioxide and naturally reduce our carbon footprint , further reducing the Earth's lifespan and overloading the atmosphere with excess greenhouse gasses. Our livelihood is severely affected by the harmful effects of carbon emissions. How can we, as a community, work together to reduce our carbon emissions?
